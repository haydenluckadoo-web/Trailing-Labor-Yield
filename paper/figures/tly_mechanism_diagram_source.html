Trailing Labor Yield (TLY)

A simple view of the mechanism: the contributor earns normal pay while active, the final active bonus is snapshotted on exit, and that snapshot tapers into the legacy payout over time.

1. While Active
Base Pay + Active Bonus
The contributor receives normal base pay each epoch and also earns an active bonus.
While the contributor remains active, the historical pay pool keeps growing.
What grows each epoch
Next historical pay pool = prior historical pool + base pay + active bonus
→
2. On Exit
Snapshot the Final Active Bonus
When the contributor leaves, the protocol locks in the final active bonus as the starting amount for the trailing claim.
What is snapshotted
Initial trailing amount = final active bonus
What is not snapshotted
The full historical pay pool is not directly paid out
→
3. Legacy State
Tapered Trailing Payout
The former contributor claims a payout each epoch. That payout tapers over a fixed duration instead of compounding forever.
How it pays
Each later claim applies the taper factor to the snapshot amount over elapsed time
Operationally
Legacy payouts are pull claims, so the treasury does not loop over alumni
The key distinction is simple: the historical pay pool compounds while a contributor stays active, but only the final active bonus becomes the basis for the trailing legacy payout.
